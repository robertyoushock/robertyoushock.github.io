Special Collections

 The photo that I choose to look into further after my trip to special collections is by photographer Lori Nix. The photos is from her 2012 collection called the city and involves a very heavily curated and designed set that goes beyond any level of basic photography or editing as she physically had to create the entire massively detailed set herself. For me personally it reminds me of a mix between the film Night at the Museum and any post-apocalyptic works you might think of. Through heavy vignetting, a heavily/oversaturated photo, as well as varying distance where you can see a city skyline but also details from the museum set right in front of you and get to see this very large landscape that is so intricate and in its nature really is really was I find just so striking and astounding about Lori Nix work here. Lori gives up this scene that is so empty and destroyed yet filled with question as to how this scene came to be- what came before and what will become of it in the future.
